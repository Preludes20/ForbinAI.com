The Five Core Domains

The Foundation of Responsible AI

H

Human Authority & Oversight

Preserves human judgment, control, and accountable decision-making.

T

Transparency

Evaluates clarity, explainability, uncertainty, and evidence.

A

Accountability

Identifies who remains responsible for AI-driven decisions and outcomes.

S

Safety

Assesses harm, misuse, unsafe reliance, and boundary failures.

P

Privacy

Protects human dignity, autonomy, information rights, and data use.

Our Methodology

A Structured, Repeatable Approach

01 DEFINE

Define the AI system, use case, governance scope, and intended impact.

02 ASSESS

Evaluate evidence, oversight, accountability, transparency, safety, and privacy.

03 MEASURE

Develop Colossus Risk Indicators™ to identify governance and trust conditions.

04 IMPROVE

Provide recommendations to strengthen responsible AI governance.

05 MONITOR

Track how trust, risk, oversight, and accountability change as AI evolves.

Why It Matters

AI Trust Is a Moving Target

AI systems are not static. Their capabilities, deployment contexts, risks, safeguards, and governance needs change over time.

Organizations need practical ways to understand whether AI systems are transparent, accountable, safe, privacy-respecting, and subject to meaningful human oversight.

Trust is not what an AI claims.
Trust is what can be independently verified.
