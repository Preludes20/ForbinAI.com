Trust

The Trust Layer Between
Humans and Artificial Intelligence

Trust is not assumed.

It is designed.

It is earned.

It must be continuously validated.

In the age of artificial intelligence, trust is not a feature of technology — it is the relationship between humans and the systems they depend on.

ForbinAI defines, measures, and strengthens that relationship through the Colossus Framework™.

The Four Questions of Trust

1

What is Trust?

Trust is the justified confidence that an AI-enabled system will act within transparent, accountable, and governed boundaries.

2

How is Trust Created?

Trust is earned through transparency, accountability, governance, meaningful human oversight, and continuous evaluation.

3

How is Trust Measured?

Trust is measured through evidence that governance is working and that outcomes align with stated intent.

4

How is Trust Lost?

Trust is lost when there is secrecy, inconsistency, lack of accountability, or failure of governance and oversight.

TRUST IS
EVIDENCE THAT

Technology can be built.
Governance can be written.
Policies can be published.

But trust is only achieved when those commitments are proven in practice, over time.

We do not claim trust.
We earn it, and we prove it.

The Colossus Trust Model™ provides the foundation for understanding how trust is established between humans and AI-enabled systems. The Colossus Framework™ provides the methodology for evaluating whether that trust is justified.
